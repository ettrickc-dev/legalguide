How to Request Online Immigration Hearing Without an Attorney

If you are handling your own immigration case and you are scared you may miss court, you cannot afford to guess. One missed hearing can create devastating damage, including an order entered without you there. If appearing in person may cause a no-show, asking for an online hearing the right way may help protect your case before it falls apart.

Yes, you can ask even if you do not have a lawyer

You do not need an attorney just to ask the immigration court for permission to appear online. EOIR’s current Respondent Access system is specifically built for respondents, and EOIR’s own hearing guidance applies to represented and self-represented people alike. Pro se respondents have hearing rights and obligations in immigration court, and EOIR provides self-help access tools for people without attorneys. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Do not make this mistake: filing nothing and hoping the judge will understand after you miss court. By then, the damage may already be done.

Why people ask for online hearings

Some people are far from the court. Some are sick. Some have childcare problems, work barriers, disability issues, transportation problems, or safety concerns. Others moved and are still stuck with a court date in another city. An online appearance request is often about one thing: avoiding a missed hearing that could wreck the case.

Strong reason: You are trying to prevent a missed court date before it happens.
Weak reason: You simply prefer online because it is easier.
Very important: The judge decides whether to allow the online appearance.

Step-by-step legal solution

1

Get your hearing notice and check the exact court, date, and time

Before you file anything, confirm where your case is set and when the hearing is scheduled. EOIR says that if you are unsure whether your hearing is in person or internet-based, you should contact the immigration court handling the case. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

2

Write a motion asking to appear online

Your request should be in writing. EOIR’s hearing guidance states that requests about remote appearance are generally made in writing and should usually be submitted about 15 days before the hearing unless the immigration judge waives that timing. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

In your motion, include:

  • Your full name
  • Your A-number
  • Your hearing date and time
  • The immigration court name
  • A clear statement that you are requesting permission to appear online or remotely
  • The exact reasons you may not be able to appear in person
  • A statement that you are making the request to avoid missing court
3

Attach proof

Judges are more likely to take a serious, documented request seriously. Attach records that match your reason. That may include medical records, job letters, childcare proof, travel records, distance proof, school records, disability documents, or anything else showing the problem is real.

4

File it through the correct EOIR process

EOIR’s Respondent Access allows respondents to file forms with the immigration court and BIA, and EOIR states that online filing tools are available for respondents. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

If you are using EOIR’s online tools, follow the court’s instructions carefully. If the court requires paper filing for your document type or situation, follow those rules exactly. A sloppy filing can fail even if your reason is good.

5

Serve the government if required

Immigration court filings often require service on DHS. If your motion needs proof of service, include it. Do not assume the judge will consider a motion that was not properly served.

6

Keep checking for the court’s response

Filing the motion does not mean it was granted. Some court materials warn that until the court approves the change, you still must appear as scheduled. That is why waiting passively is dangerous. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

7

Have a backup plan in case the judge says no

This part matters. An online-hearing request is a request, not a guarantee. If the judge denies it or never rules before the hearing, you may still need to appear in person to protect yourself from a missed-hearing disaster.

Bottom line: if you do not have a lawyer, you can still ask for an online hearing. The key is to do it early, in writing, with proof, and without assuming the request automatically excuses your appearance.

What your motion should say

Your motion should sound respectful, specific, and urgent. It should explain that you are trying to comply with the court, not avoid the case. That difference matters.

Core points to include

  • You are self-represented and are requesting leave to appear remotely
  • You want to avoid missing your hearing
  • You are willing to appear using the court’s approved online system
  • Your reason is real and supported by documents
  • You are asking the court to protect orderly attendance, not delay the case
Weak requests get ignored. Strong requests explain the risk clearly and show you are trying to obey the court.

Big mistakes to avoid

  • Waiting until the last minute
  • Sending an informal message instead of a real written motion
  • Giving no proof
  • Assuming online appearance is automatic
  • Failing to update your address if you moved
  • Thinking a request to appear online is the same as changing venue

EOIR separately requires respondents to update address changes quickly, generally within five working days, and warns that until a motion to change venue is decided, the person still must appear at all scheduled hearings. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

When this page matters most

  • Your hearing is coming soon and travel may make you miss court
  • You do not have an attorney and need to handle the request yourself
  • You moved and the court is far away
  • You are sick or have a medical barrier
  • You have childcare, job, or school barriers that may cause a no-show
  • You are afraid missing court could lead to deportation problems

FAQ

Can I ask for an online immigration hearing without a lawyer?

Yes. EOIR provides respondent-facing filing access and self-help tools, and pro se respondents have hearing rights and obligations in court. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

How early should I file the request?

EOIR’s hearing guidance says filings requesting a ruling before a hearing are generally due about 15 days before the hearing, unless the judge waives that timing or the court sets something different. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Does filing the motion mean I can stay home?

No. Until the court grants the request or changes the hearing format, you should not assume you are excused from appearing as originally scheduled. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Is asking for an online hearing the same as moving my case?

No. A request to appear remotely is different from a motion to change venue. EOIR treats venue issues separately. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Do not let “I don’t have a lawyer” turn into a missed hearing

You do not need to wait until the damage is done. If getting to court in person may cause you to miss your hearing, act now. A properly prepared request to appear online may help protect your case before it spirals into something much harder to fix.
