Consent vs. Default — Two Different Packets

New York has two versions of the uncontested divorce packet. Which one you use depends entirely on whether your spouse cooperates. Using the wrong version — or mixing documents from both — is one of the most common reasons filings get rejected.

📋 Consent Packet — Spouse Signs

  • Summons & Verified Complaint (Consent version)
  • Affidavit of Plaintiff
  • Affidavit of Defendant
  • Health Insurance Affidavit — Plaintiff
  • Health Insurance Affidavit — Defendant
  • Note of Issue — WAIVER version
  • Affirmation of Regularity — Consent
  • Findings of Fact — Consent
  • Judgment of Divorce — Consent
  • Part 130 Certification
Use when: your spouse agrees and will sign papers

📋 Default Packet — Spouse Served

  • Summons & Verified Complaint (Default version)
  • Affidavit of Plaintiff
  • Affidavit of Service
  • Health Insurance Affidavit — Plaintiff
  • Sworn Statement of Removal of Barriers
  • Note of Issue — DEFAULT version
  • Affirmation of Regularity — Default
  • Findings of Fact — Default
  • Judgment of Divorce — Default
  • Part 130 Certification
Use when: spouse was served but did not respond

🚨 Top Mistakes That Get NY Divorce Packets Rejected

  • Using Consent documents when you have a Default situation — or vice versa
  • Not writing the Index Number on every single document after you get it from the clerk
  • Signing the Verified Complaint before going to the notary — must sign in front of them
  • Using the wrong Note of Issue version (Waiver vs. Default)
  • Forgetting the Part 130 Certification — very commonly left out
  • Leaving Social Security Number fields blank — must write NONE if none exists
  • Not making 3 copies of the Note of Issue
  • Signing the judge or clerk sections of Findings/Judgment — leave those blank

The Exact Sequence — In Order

The order matters. Filing things out of sequence or signing before the right time causes rejections.

1
Notarize your Verified Complaint

Go to a notary with the unsigned Complaint. Sign the verification in front of them. They stamp and sign. This is your first notarized document.

2
File Summons & Complaint at County Clerk — get Index Number

Pay ~$210. Immediately write the Index Number in the top right corner of every single document in your packet. Do this before you leave the clerk's window.

3
Consent: Give spouse papers to sign. Default: Have spouse served.

Consent: give your spouse the Affidavit of Defendant + Health Insurance Affidavit (Defendant) to complete and notarize. Default: have a third party (not you, 18+) personally serve your spouse with the Summons & Complaint and all required notices.

4
Default only: Wait 40 days after service

You must give your spouse 20 days to respond if served in NY (30 days if served out of state), plus additional time. After 40 days total with no response, you can proceed.

5
Notarize your Affidavit of Plaintiff + Health Insurance Affidavit

Go back to the notary. Both must be signed in front of the notary — not before.

6
Complete Note of Issue, Affirmation, Findings, Judgment, Certification

Add Index Number to each. Fill in filing date on Findings. Add SSNs on Findings and Judgment (write NONE if none). Do NOT sign judge or clerk lines.

7
File final packet at County Clerk — pay ~$125

Bring 3 copies of Note of Issue. Clerk gives you RJI and Certificate of Dissolution. Complete and return them. Wait for judge to sign your Judgment of Divorce.
