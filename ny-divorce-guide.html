New York Divorce Requirements

To file for divorce in New York, at least one spouse must meet the residency requirement: you must have lived in New York for at least 2 consecutive years immediately before filing, OR you must have been married in New York and at least one spouse has been a resident for at least 1 year before filing, OR the grounds for divorce arose in New York and at least one spouse is a current resident.

Grounds for Divorce in New York

New York allows both no-fault and fault-based divorce. The most common ground used today is no-fault: irretrievable breakdown of the marriage for at least six months. Fault grounds include cruel and inhuman treatment, abandonment for one year or more, imprisonment for three or more years, and adultery.

Uncontested vs. Contested Divorce

An uncontested divorce is one where both spouses agree on all issues — division of property, support, and custody if children are involved. This is significantly faster and cheaper. A contested divorce involves disputes that must be resolved by the court, which can take years and cost tens of thousands of dollars in attorney fees.
