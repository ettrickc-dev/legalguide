What LegalZoom's $499 Gets You — and What It Doesn't

LegalZoom's divorce document service prepares a set of divorce forms based on your answers to an intake questionnaire. For New York, this means:

  • You get: A Summons, Verified Complaint, and supporting documents
  • You don't get: NY-specific documents like the Affirmation of Regularity, the county-specific Note of Issue, or the Sworn Statement of Removal of Barriers to Remarriage that New York courts require
  • You don't get: Coverage for default divorce (when your spouse doesn't respond) — LegalZoom only covers consent divorce
  • You don't get: Personalized step-by-step instructions for your specific county
  • The documents are prepared by: CompleteCase.com, a third-party service that LegalZoom outsources to

What the ~$335 New York Court Filing Fee Covers

This fee is paid directly to the New York courthouse — not to any document service. No company can waive it. It covers:

  • ~$210: Index Number (filing fee when you start your case at the County Clerk)
  • ~$125: Second filing fee when you submit your final divorce packet

The court fees are the same regardless of whether you use LegalZoom, EasyLegalFiling, a free service, or an attorney.
