In a consent divorce, your spouse agrees to the divorce, signs an Affidavit of Defendant, and waives their right to contest. In a default divorce, your spouse does not respond after being properly served — you proceed without their cooperation. Both result in a Judgment of Divorce. The consent route is faster; the default route requires a 40-day waiting period after service.
After you file your final packet with the court, the judge typically reviews and signs the Judgment of Divorce within 3–6 months, depending on the county and the court's current workload. Some counties are faster. Kings, Queens, and New York County tend to take longer than suburban counties.
A small estate affidavit (formally called an Affidavit in Relation to Voluntary Administration) is a simplified court process under SCPA §1310 for estates where the total personal property is under $50,000 and there is no real estate solely in the decedent's name. It is faster and less expensive than full probate or administration. Use our free inheritance rights tool to find out if this applies to your situation.
