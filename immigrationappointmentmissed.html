Missed Immigration Appointment? Here’s What Happens Next

You cannot afford to ignore this.

Missing an immigration appointment — whether with USCIS or immigration court — can trigger serious consequences, including delays, denial of your application, or even removal (deportation) proceedings.

⚠️ Every day you wait makes your situation worse. Some cases can be fixed — but only if you act quickly.
---

What Happens If You Miss an Immigration Appointment?

The consequences depend on the type of appointment you missed, but here’s what typically happens:

  • USCIS Appointment (Biometrics / Interview): Your case may be delayed or denied
  • Immigration Court Hearing: A removal order may be issued against you
  • InfoPass or Other Requests: You may need to reschedule, but delays can hurt your case

In many situations, missing one appointment can put your entire case at risk.

---

Worst Case Scenario (And Why You Must Act Fast)

If you missed a court hearing, the judge may:

  • Issue an order of removal (deportation)
  • Close your case permanently
  • Prevent you from applying for future relief
⚠️ Many people don’t even realize they’ve been ordered deported until it’s too late.
---

Can You Fix It?

Yes — but only if you act immediately.

You may be able to:

  • File a motion to reopen your case
  • Reschedule your missed appointment
  • Explain your absence with valid proof

However, success depends on:

  • How quickly you act
  • Your reason for missing the appointment
  • Whether proper legal documents are filed
---

Step-by-Step: What You Should Do Right Now

Step 1 — Find out what you missed
Check your notice. Was it USCIS or immigration court?

Step 2 — Do NOT ignore it
Ignoring the issue will almost always make it worse.

Step 3 — Gather proof
Medical records, address issues, emergencies — anything that explains why you missed it.

Step 4 — Take action immediately
You may need to file a motion or request quickly to protect your case.

---

What NOT To Do

  • ❌ Do NOT wait “to see what happens”
  • ❌ Do NOT assume it will fix itself
  • ❌ Do NOT miss a second appointment

Each delay reduces your chances of fixing the problem.

---

Important: Deadlines Matter

Some motions and requests must be filed within strict time limits.

If you miss these deadlines, you may lose your ability to fix your case entirely.
