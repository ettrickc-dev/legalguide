Can You Attend Immigration Court Online Instead of In Person?

Some people assume they can just log in from home. That mistake can wreck a case. Immigration court does use internet-based hearings, but self-represented respondents usually default to in-person unless the court allows something different. If travel, illness, work, childcare, or safety issues may cause a missed hearing, the time to act is before the court date.

What matters most

Yes, immigration court can be online in some situations, but you should not assume your hearing is remote unless the court says so.

Urgent warning: the biggest mistake is assuming the court will forgive a missed hearing because you meant to appear online. If you are not sure of the hearing format, fix that before the court date.

Step-by-step legal solution

1

Check your hearing notice first

Confirm the date, time, and court from your hearing notice. If you are unsure whether your hearing is in person or internet-based, contact the immigration court handling the case and check your case information online.

2

Prepare a written request or motion

Do not rely on a casual call or guess. Put the request in writing, explain why in-person attendance may cause a missed hearing, and ask the judge to allow online or remote appearance.

3

Attach proof

Support your request with documents that match your reason. Medical proof, travel barriers, childcare records, job letters, and distance proof can make the request more credible.

4

File early enough for a ruling

Pre-hearing filings that request a ruling generally should be filed early enough for the court to act. Waiting until the last minute is one of the biggest reasons these requests fail.

5

Serve DHS if required

If your filing needs proof of service on the government, do not skip that step. A filing can fail even when the reason is strong if the procedure is not followed.

6

Do not assume the request was granted

Until the court approves the request or changes the hearing format, treat the original hearing as real and dangerous to miss.

Bottom line: the safest path is to act early, file properly, support the request with proof, and protect yourself against a missed hearing while the court decides.

When online attendance may matter most

  • You live far from the court and travel is a real barrier
  • You are sick, disabled, or dealing with a medical issue
  • You are afraid missing court could trigger an order entered without you
  • You need to ask early instead of trying to fix the damage later

Big mistake to avoid

Do not assume: because EOIR posts Webex links for judges, every respondent can choose remote appearance on their own. If the court has not approved your remote appearance, treat the hearing seriously as scheduled.

FAQ

Can you just choose online appearance on your own?

No. Immigration courts use internet-based hearings, but you should not assume you can appear remotely unless the court allows it.

How early should you file the request?

The safer approach is well before the hearing, because EOIR practice guidance generally expects pre-hearing filings that request a ruling about 15 days before the hearing.

Does filing the motion excuse your hearing?

No. Until the court grants the request, do not assume you are excused from appearing as originally scheduled.

Is remote appearance the same as changing venue?

No. Asking to appear online is different from asking to transfer the case to another immigration court.

Do not wait until a hearing mistake turns into a deportation problem

If you are searching for help on can you attend immigration court online, your case may already be in a risky zone. Take the hearing seriously, act before the deadline, and do not assume the court knows your problem unless you tell it the right way.
