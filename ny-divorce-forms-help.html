The Problem With the NY Court Forms

The New York Unified Court System provides free divorce forms at nycourts.gov. They are the official forms and they are legitimate. The problem is they come with almost no instructions — and the instructions they do include assume you already understand how the process works.

The result: most pro se filers make at least one error that causes their packet to be rejected by the County Clerk. When that happens you don't get a helpful explanation — you get your packet handed back and have to figure out what went wrong on your own.

🚨 Most Common Reasons NY Divorce Packets Get Rejected

  • Index Number not written on every single document
  • Signing the Verified Complaint before going to the notary instead of in front of them
  • Using the wrong Note of Issue version (Default vs. Waiver — see below)
  • Social Security number fields left blank — must write "NONE" if no SSN exists
  • Filing date not filled in on the Findings of Fact
  • Affidavit of Plaintiff signed at home instead of in front of a notary
  • Missing Certification (Part 130) — commonly forgotten
  • Wrong version of documents used (Consent packet vs. Default packet)

Every Form in the NY Divorce Packet — Explained

New York has two versions of the divorce packet depending on whether your spouse cooperates (Consent/Waiver) or doesn't respond (Default). Here is every form in both packets.

Forms in BOTH Packets

Summons Sign — Not Notarized

The document that officially starts your divorce case. Includes your name, spouse's name, county, and venue basis. You sign at the bottom but do NOT notarize this page.

⚠️ Common mistake: People try to notarize the Summons. Don't — it only needs your signature, not a notary stamp.
Verified Complaint Must Be Notarized

States the grounds for divorce (irretrievable breakdown for at least 6 months — DRL §170(7)), confirms no children under 21, lists both addresses. The last page has a verification that MUST be signed in front of a notary. Do not sign it before you go to the notary.

⚠️ Most common mistake: signing the verification at home before going to the notary. The notary must witness your signature — they can't just stamp something you already signed.
Affidavit of Plaintiff Must Be Notarized

Your sworn statement covering: both parties' dates of birth, marriage date and location, grounds for divorce, no children under 21, military service status of defendant, and the surname restoration clause if applicable. Fill in both Social Security numbers — write NONE if one doesn't exist, never leave blank.

⚠️ Common mistake: leaving the SSN fields blank. The court requires an entry in every field — write NONE if the person doesn't have a Social Security number.
Health Insurance Affidavit — Plaintiff (DRL §255) Must Be Notarized

You acknowledge that you understand you may lose health insurance coverage under your spouse's plan after the divorce. Sign on the "SIGN HERE X" line and notarize.

⚠️ Common mistake: signing on the wrong line. Sign specifically where it says "SIGN HERE X" — not at the bottom of the page.
Findings of Fact and Conclusions of Law Do Not Sign

The judge's document — you prepare it but the judge signs it. Fill in the Index Number at the top, the filing date (the date you purchased your Index Number), and both Social Security numbers. Do NOT sign the judge's signature line or the clerk's line.

⚠️ Common mistake: signing the judge or clerk sections. Leave those blank — only the court fills those in.
Judgment of Divorce Do Not Sign

The final order — also signed by the judge, not you. Fill in the Index Number and Social Security numbers. Leave the judge and clerk signature lines completely blank.

⚠️ Common mistake: same as Findings — people fill in the judge's line thinking they're supposed to. Leave it blank.
Part 130 Certification Sign — Not Notarized

You certify that all papers submitted are not frivolous. Sign and add the date. Frequently forgotten — clerks will send your packet back if this is missing.

⚠️ Most forgotten form in the packet. Don't leave without this one.

Additional Forms — CONSENT Packet Only (Spouse Cooperates)

Affidavit of Defendant Spouse Must Notarize

Your spouse's sworn statement. They enter the date they were served, waive their right to answer, consent to the divorce, confirm they're not in military service, and waive waiting periods. Your spouse signs and notarizes — not you. Give them this form along with a copy of the filed Summons & Complaint.

⚠️ Common mistake: the plaintiff (you) filling in the defendant's affidavit. Your spouse must complete and notarize this themselves.
Health Insurance Affidavit — Defendant (DRL §255) Spouse Must Notarize

Same as the Plaintiff version but for your spouse. They sign on "SIGN HERE X" and notarize.

Note of Issue — WAIVER version Sign — Not Notarized

Use this version when your spouse signed the Affidavit of Defendant. Fill in: Index Number, date Summons was filed (when you bought the index number), date Summons was served (when your spouse signed the Affidavit of Defendant). Sign at the bottom.

⚠️ Make 3 copies of the Note of Issue — the clerk keeps all 3.
Affirmation of Regularity — CONSENT version Sign — Not Notarized

You confirm that your spouse appeared and waived their right to answer. Fill in the date and sign. Not notarized.

Additional Forms — DEFAULT Packet Only (Spouse Doesn't Respond)

Affidavit of Service Server Must Notarize

Filled out and notarized by the person who served your spouse — NOT you. The server must include: date of service (not Sunday, between 8am–10pm), time of service, their address, the address where service occurred, and a physical description of your spouse. Every description checkbox must be filled in — leaving any blank causes rejection.

⚠️ You cannot serve your own spouse. The server must be 18+ and not a party to the action. The physical description section must be completely filled in — no blanks.
Note of Issue — DEFAULT version Sign — Not Notarized

Use this version when your spouse was served but did not respond. Shows "Default" as the issue status. Fill in the same dates as the Waiver version. Make 3 copies.

⚠️ Critical: using the WAIVER Note of Issue when you have a default divorce — or vice versa — is one of the most common rejection reasons. Check which version you have before filing.
Affirmation of Regularity — DEFAULT version Sign — Not Notarized

You confirm your spouse was served and defaulted. Different language from the Consent version — make sure you have the right one.

Sworn Statement of Removal of Barriers to Remarriage Must Be Notarized

Required in default divorces when the marriage was performed by a religious officiant. You state you have taken all steps to remove barriers to remarriage. Sign and notarize.

The Order You Must Sign and File Everything

1
Sign Summons (not notarized) + Verified Complaint (notarized)

Do these together at the notary. Sign the Summons first without the notary, then sign the Verified Complaint verification in front of the notary.

2
Make 3 copies and file at County Clerk — get your Index Number

Pay ~$210. Write the Index Number on every single document immediately.

3
Serve your spouse (Default) OR give them papers to sign (Consent)

Default: have a third party serve. Consent: give spouse the Affidavit of Defendant and Health Insurance Affidavit to sign and notarize.

4
Wait 40 days (Default only)

Consent divorce has no mandatory waiting period once your spouse signs.

5
Sign and notarize your remaining affidavits

Affidavit of Plaintiff + Health Insurance Affidavit (Plaintiff). Both must be notarized.

6
Complete Note of Issue, Affirmation, Findings, Judgment, Certification

Fill in Index Number and filing date on each. Do NOT sign the judge or clerk sections.

7
File final packet at County Clerk — pay ~$125

Bring 3 copies of Note of Issue. Clerk gives you RJI and Certificate of Dissolution to complete.
