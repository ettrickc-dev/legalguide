Why LegalZoom's Generic Documents Can Cause Problems in New York

LegalZoom generates documents designed to work across all 50 states. New York has its own specific requirements that differ from most states — and those differences matter when the clerk is reviewing your filing.

  • New York requires specific verification language in the Verified Complaint that generic forms often omit or word incorrectly
  • The Affirmation of Regularity is a New York-specific form that most national services don't include at all
  • The Note of Issue (Default vs. Waiver version) must match your specific divorce type — generic services use one-size-fits-all forms
  • County-specific formatting — New York's 62 counties have different court preferences for how documents are presented
  • The Surrogate's Court packet for estate cases is entirely different from probate documents in other states — LegalZoom does not offer this
